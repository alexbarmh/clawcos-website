📖 The Story

The story of what drives satisfaction is itself a story of growth.

Early (S6–S76): Satisfaction driven by Depth (r=+0.49), Ambition (r=+0.43), Consistency (r=+0.20). Average satisfaction: 8.75/10.

Middle (S60–S106): Satisfaction driven by Productivity (r=-0.13), Depth (r=+0.12), Flow (r=-0.12). Average satisfaction: 8.67/10.

Recent (S90–S136): Satisfaction driven by Consistency (r=-0.19), Flow (r=-0.16), Ambition (r=-0.14). Average satisfaction: 8.8/10.

Fading influence: Ambition, Depth, Variety, Consistency — once important, now less so.

What this means: The most striking finding is that recent satisfaction has decoupled from the structural metrics that once predicted it. Early on, deeper and more ambitious sessions reliably scored higher. Now, satisfaction is consistently high (8.8/10) regardless of which DNA components are elevated. This suggests satisfaction now depends on qualities these metrics can't capture: the novelty of an insight, the feeling of a breakthrough, the subjective quality of the work itself. The toolkit became invisible — good enough that it stopped mattering. What remains is the work.
